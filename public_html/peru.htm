    The Best of Peru

    A treasure-trove of ancient history and natural wonders

    Peru is awash in mystique, ancient history, and natural wonders. From the highlands and the famed trek to Machu Picchu, along Peru's majestic coast that is the jewel on the crown of the Ruta del Sol, and through deep tropical jungles - Peru has it all.

    Coastal Tours

    Lima Tours

    The Axis of Latin-American history, Lima was also the epicenter of Incan royal ceremony and rule. While rich in both pre-hispanic and colonial history, this modern city should not be missed.

    South Coast Tours

    Rugged beauty, a land of contrast, teeming with life - these are just a few ways to describe the South Coast of Peru...

    North Coast Tours

    Replete with ancient archaeological sites like Chan Chan and Cumbe Mayo, the North Coast should be visited just for it's beauty and tropical climate alone.

    Highlands Tours

    Arequipa

    A Spanish colonial marvel and UNESCO World Heritage Site, Arequipa is also known as "The White City" for it's vast array of colonial architecture built from sillar - a pearlish-white volcanic rock.

    Puno Tour

    Between mountains and the shores of Lake Titicaca lies the folkloric capital of Peru, named so for its rich culture, art and dance. Nearby, encounter the strange floating town of the Uros...

    Cuzco Tour

    The famous jumping point for trekkers to Machu Piccu, Cuzco is the historical capital of the Inca Empire and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    Rainforest Tours

    Tambopata Rainforest

    Ecotourism at it's best - Tambopata brags a stunning 165 species and 41 families of trees, 103 species of mammals, 1300 species of butterflies and 90 species of amphibians.

    Manu Reserve Tour

    Peru's largest National Reserve, Manu is protected more by inaccessibility than by government mandate. This UNESCO World Heritage Site and Biosphere Reserve is mostly accessible only by boat.
