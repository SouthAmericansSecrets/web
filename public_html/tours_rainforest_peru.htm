    Lima City Tour

    Axis of Latin American History

    The best introduction to Peruvian natural history and it's three historical periods: pre-Hispanic, colonial, and contemporary. Lima was the home pre-Colombian cultures which date back thousands of years. The land of the Incas, Lima was conquered by Spaniards on the 18th of January, 1535. Tour with South Americans' Secrets to learn and retrace the steps of this millennial history.

    Discover ancestral Lima "Huaca Pucllana" - the Incan's most important ceremonial and administrative center and South America's most culturally developed civilization between 200 AD and 700 AD. Marvel at the great adobe clay pyramid constructed during this period with it's seven staggering platforms.

    Colonial Lima, preserved in the Lima's historic center, is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Peru and South America. Located principally in downtown Lima, it has been known as the City of Kings for millenia. This heritage has earned Unesco's moniker as World Heritage Site, namely for it's originality and high concentration of historic monuments constructed during Spanish rule.

    During the era of the Vice-Royal of Peru, a work of art was created representing the magnificence and, grandeur of the legendary royal life. Our trip includes the Paseo de la Republica (Promenade of the Republic), Plaza San Martin (San Martin Square), Plaza Mayor (Main Square), Palacio de Gobierno (Government Palace), Palacio Arzobispal (Archbishop's palace), Basilica Catedral (Basilica Cathedral), Palacio Municipal (Municipal Palace), and the Convent of San Francisco which still holds many colonial treasures including the subterranean catacombs of San Francisco.

    Contemporary Lima boasts all the modern world has to offer and the tour is complete with a visit to the modern district of San Isidro with a stop at Olive Grove then a visit to Miraflores and the fantastic Larco Mar shopping center that is built on cliffs overlooking the ocean.

    Pachacamac

    A unique archaeological and mystical experience

    This guided tour of the vast and splendid archaeological citadel of Pachacamac, the temple of the deity, and Pachacamac - believed to be the creator of the universe and a divinity adored by old Andean civilizations. A sacred and ceremonial site dating back to the beginnings of Christianity where thousands of pilgrims converged to pay tribute to and consult the Pachacamac Oracle who they believed could see the past, the future, and the fate of any man, woman, or child.

    The site comprises pyramidal temples and enclosures built by successive pre-Colombian civilizations then finally the Incas. Highlights include the Temple of Pachacamac, the Sun God Shrine, and the Palace of the Chosen Women or Virgins of the Sun. At the site's museum find an idol of the deity Pachacamac, which was invisible to the profane, and was reserved for the high priests who kept, revered, and interpreted the Divine Oracle in old times. A sacred site, the temple was considered to be part of the planet’s mystical axis.

    Caral

    The Oldest Civilization of America

    For those with a keen interest in archeology and adventure, the tour embarks from Lima via the Ruta del Sol (just around 2 hours) enroute to discover the Norte Central civilization.

    Also known as Caral or Caral-Supe, the Norte Central civilization was a complex Pre-Columbian society that spanned nearly 30 major population centers in what is now known as the Norte Chico region of north-central coastal Peru.

    The oldest known civilization in the Americas and one of six sites where civilization separately originated in the ancient world, Caral flourished between 3000 BC and 1800 BC. It's derivative name, Caral-Supe, is born from Caral in the Supe Valley, a large and significantly studied site in Norte Chico. Complex society in Norte Chico emerged just a millennium after Sumer in Mesopotamia, around the same time as the pyramids of Ancient Egypt. Norte Chico predates the Mesoamerican Olmec by nearly two millennia.

    Caral was discovered in 1948 but received little attention until an archaeologist named Ruth Shady further explored the 5,000 year-old city of pyramids in the Peruvian desert with its elaborate complex of temples, amphitheater, and ordinary houses. The urban complex is spread out over 150 acres (607,000 m²) and contains several plazas and residential buildings and indicates that Caral was a thriving metropolis at approximately the same time that Egypt's great pyramids were being erected.

    The Caral tour is a full day leaving early from Lima and returning late afternoon. South Americans' Secrets arranges visits to the area with professional tour

    Peruvian Gold

    See Peru’s most famous gold collection, a vast and dazzling array of ancient Peruvian gold and the Arms of the World Collection.

    Ancient Peruvians were master metalworkers. The “cire perdue” or lost wax process, known across the Orient and lost in the West until the Renaissance, was the most frequent technique used by master ancient Peruvian gold and silversmiths.

    On our tour of Peru’s Gold Museum, visitors will admire an awe-inspiring collection of gold artifacts from various pre-Colombian civilizations, some more than 3,000 years old.

    Metal sculptures and jewelery of immense value in gold, silver, and precious stones, crafted for the children of the Sun God and monarchs of the vast Inca Empire, are on display for your amazement. These are the remnants that attracted so many Spanish conquerors and European adventurers who, in their quest of Peruvian gold, broke the back of the Incan civilization and later founded the Vice-Royalty of Peru under the Spanish Crown.

    Peru’s Gold Museum also displays a valuable collection of old and modern weapons that ranks among the best in the world. The halls in this section show the Arms of the World Collection of armor, uniforms and various ancient war artifacts and weapons from various time periods around the world, some dating back to as long ago as 1300 B.C.

    The tour also includes a visit to Friendship Park.

    Museums of Lima

    Enjoy visits to Larco Museum and Archaeological Museum in a tour that comprises breathtaking colonial mansions housing probably the best gold, ceramic, textile, and art collections in South America.

    These two museums house incomparable artifacts and collections of pre-Colombian art from Peru and the New World and are, arguably, the most important museums in South America.

    Larco Museum is housed in a unique colonial residence dating back to the 17th century and built atop a pyramid of the 8th century. This fabulous museum includes an exquisite collection of Peruvian gold and silver from the ancient Incas. It also hosts a pre-Hispanic erotic art salon. Its over 45,000 archaeological pieces, classified by renowned specialists and scholars, tour major world museums regularly. Larco is among only a few museums around the world opening their vaults to the public and can boast that it exhibits the largest private collection of pre-Colombian Peruvian art in the world.

    The National Museum of Archaeology, Anthropology and History, also housed in an old Colonial mansion, was formerly the residence of Peru’s viceroys, then of its liberators. In this, Peru’s grandest museum, you find the Estela Raymondi and Tello Obelisk monuments - carved stone pieces dating back to the Formative period over three thousand years ago. The museum also features the largest textile collection, with fabrics from the Pre-Ceramic period through the Inca Empire, and a major collection of Ancient Peruvian metals. And the impressive Tawantinsuyo Room illustrates one hundred years of the Inca Empire through virtual models and re-creations.
