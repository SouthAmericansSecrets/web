Non-human DNA Found in Paracas Skulls

“Whatever the sample labeled 3A has came from – it had mtDNA with mutations unknown in any human, primate or animal known so far” Geneticist

March 1, 2014 - Translated by Rein Petersen

In 1928 Julio Tello discovered an ancient graveyard containing tombs filled with the remains of “humans” with elongated skulls, the famous Paracas Skulls.

Estimated to be over 3000 years old, a recent DNA analysis has suggested they may not be human, but rather a completely new species. This is according to Brien Foerster who is a researcher and assistant director of the Paracas Museum.
